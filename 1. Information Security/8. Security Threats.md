# 🔐 Security Threats Explained

## 📖 Introduction

Security threats are activities, attacks, or events that can compromise the confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA Triad) of information systems. Understanding these threats is essential for protecting networks, applications, and sensitive data.

---

## 🎯 CIA Triad Impact

| Principle          | Description                                     |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------------------- |
| 🔒 Confidentiality | Protecting information from unauthorized access |
| 🔧 Integrity       | Ensuring data remains accurate and unmodified   |
| ⚙️ Availability    | Ensuring systems and services remain accessible |

---

# 1️⃣ Malware (Malicious Software)

Malware refers to software intentionally designed to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to systems.

### Common Types

* 🦠 Virus
* 🕸️ Worm
* 🔐 Ransomware
* 🎭 Trojan Horse
* 👁️ Spyware & Adware

### Impact

* Data loss
* Financial damage
* System compromise

---

# 2️⃣ Social Engineering

Social engineering attacks manipulate human behavior to obtain sensitive information.

### Techniques

* 📧 Phishing
* 📞 Vishing
* 🎭 Pretexting
* 🎁 Baiting

### Impact

* Credential theft
* Unauthorized access
* Financial fraud

---

# 3️⃣ Insider Threats

Threats originating from individuals within an organization.

### Categories

* 🚨 Malicious Insider
* ⚠️ Negligent Insider

### Risks

* Data theft
* Information leakage
* Operational disruption

---

# 4️⃣ Unauthorized Access

Attackers attempt to gain access to systems without permission.

### Methods

* 🔑 Brute Force Attacks
* 📂 Credential Stuffing
* ⬆️ Privilege Escalation

### Impact

* Account compromise
* Data exposure
* System takeover

---

# 5️⃣ DoS & DDoS Attacks

Denial of Service attacks aim to make services unavailable.

### Types

* 🚫 DoS (Single Source)
* 🌊 DDoS (Multiple Sources)

### Impact

* Service downtime
* Revenue loss
* Reputation damage

---

# 6️⃣ Data Breaches

Unauthorized exposure or theft of confidential information.

### Consequences

* Sensitive data leakage
* Regulatory penalties
* Loss of customer trust

---

# 7️⃣ Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs)

Sophisticated, long-term attacks targeting valuable information.

### Characteristics

* 🎯 Targeted attacks
* 🕵️ Stealthy operations
* ⏳ Long-term persistence

---

# 8️⃣ Vulnerability Exploitation

Attackers exploit security weaknesses in systems.

### Examples

* ⚡ Zero-Day Exploits
* ⚙️ Security Misconfigurations
* 📦 Unpatched Software

---

# 9️⃣ Man-in-the-Middle (MitM) Attacks

Attackers intercept communication between two parties.

### Examples

* Network eavesdropping
* Session hijacking
* HTTPS spoofing

---

# 🔟 IoT Security Threats

Internet-connected devices often have weak security controls.

### Risks

* Weak passwords
* Outdated firmware
* Botnet recruitment

---

# 1️⃣1️⃣ Supply Chain Attacks

Compromising trusted vendors or software providers.

### Examples

* Malicious software updates
* Third-party service compromise

---

# 1️⃣2️⃣ Cloud Security Threats

Threats affecting cloud-hosted resources and services.

### Common Issues

* Public storage exposure
* Insecure APIs
* Misconfigured permissions

---

# 1️⃣3️⃣ Emerging Threats

Modern and evolving cyber threats.

### Examples

* 🤖 AI-Powered Attacks
* 🎭 Deepfakes
* ⚛️ Quantum Computing Risks

---

| Vulnerability Exploits | System takeover            |
| MitM Attacks           | Communication interception |

---

## 🚀 Conclusion

Security threats continue to evolve as technology advances. Organizations and individuals must implement strong security practices, regular monitoring, employee awareness training, and timely patch management to reduce cyber risk and maintain a strong security posture.
